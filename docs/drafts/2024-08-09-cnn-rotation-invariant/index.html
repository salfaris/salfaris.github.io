“Why is a convolutional neural network (CNN) not rotation invariant?”

This question popped up in an interview recently, and it is actually deeper than I initially thought because there are some caveats to making this statement, and you would only know them if you have a solid understanding on the convolutional net architecture. For a start, you have to define precisely what do you mean by a convolutional neural net. Because last time I check, a convolutional neural net… can be made to be rotation invariant.

With the caveats put in place, I was able to reason why a convolutional net is not rotation invariant when answering the question, but deep inside, I was not happy to not be able to prove it mathematically in my head. I have also yet to find a single article that give me a convincing argument for why this statement is true. So today, I am to putting this question to bed with a somewhat acceptable mathematical reasoning.

Let’s get one important thing out of the way first because this might confuse some people. The statement “convolutional net is not rotation invariant” is somewhat misleading; and this is a consequence of people not understanding the difference betwen a convolutional neural net, a convolutional layer and a convolutional operator.

So what is actually “convolution” in a convolutional neural net?

A convolutional neural network is simply a neural network where at least one of its layers utilize the convolutional operator rather than a full-blown matrix multiplication (Goodfellow, Bengio, and Courville 2016). This definition is so general that it renders the statement “why is a convolutional neural network (CNN) not rotation invariant?” to be false, especially with our breadth of knowledge in deep learning today. I will demonstrate why precisely below.

To jog your memory, a convolution of two functions f and g is the integral transform

(f * g)(t) = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f(\tau) g(t - \tau) d\tau.

(deeplearning2016?) took this definition further and define

The convolutional operator is an affine transformation that is not rotation invariant.

does not imply that a convolutional net cannot learn filters that recognize rotation of an image. It means that the convolutional operator is not rotation invariant
